Welcome to the <strong style="box-sizing:border-box;font-weight:bolder;padding-top:0px;">Tay5G Challenge Fund 2</strong> webinar. This is a great chance to find out more about applying and get some insights on eligible ideas. The Tay Cities Deal Tay5G Challenge Fund 2 is a UK-wide grant funding opportunity worth up to Â£600,000 supported by the Scottish Government. Funding is available for projects that demonstrate and validate innovative products and services for use, adoption or scaling up of 5G supported solutions. Tay5G is led by Dundee City Council and is part of Tay Cities Deal, a programme of regional strategic, social and economic development projects for Tay Cities Region (TCR), supported by the Scottish and UK Governments. TCR takes in the Councils areas of Angus, Dundee, Fife and Perth & Kinross. Project leads and any partners must be UK registered organisations but need not be based in TCR. <a style="background-color:transparent;box-sizing:border-box;color:var(--eds-control,#3659e3);text-decoration:none;" href="https://www.taycities.co.uk/" title="https://www.taycities.co.uk/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Read more about Tay Cities Deal (opens Tay Cities Deal website).</a>

For more information please go to <a style="background-color:transparent;box-sizing:border-box;color:var(--eds-control,#3659e3);padding-top:0px;text-decoration:none;" href="https://www.digitaldundee.com/tay5g/tay5g-challenge-fund-2" title="https://www.digitaldundee.com/tay5g/tay5g-challenge-fund-2" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Tay5G Challenge Fund 2</a>.

For more information on 5G and previous use case trials across Scotland please take a look at the <a style="background-color:transparent;box-sizing:border-box;color:var(--eds-control,#3659e3);padding-top:0px;text-decoration:none;" href="https://scotland5gcentre.org/" title="https://scotland5gcentre.org/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Scotland 5G Centre</a>.

The webinar will be presented by Dundee City Council, that leads on the Tay5G project and the Scotland 5G Centre, a national centre funded by the Scottish Government. It supports the delivery of advanced communication solutions to show how valuable 5G can be to organisations and the economy as a whole.
